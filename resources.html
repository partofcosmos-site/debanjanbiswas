A raw compilation of my real experiences, preparation strategies, and resources I have been exposed to during my high school journey (updated as of July 5, 2026).

Your approach to I.E. Irodov's Problems in General Physics should depend entirely on your target objective:

Scenario A: Having Real Fun

If your goal is falling in love with physics, there is no set system. Just go ahead, try solving them, fail, come back, review your theory, and use AI or online resources to speed up your learning. Never compromise on depth—the process is about building deep intuition.

Scenario B: Under Time Constraints

If you are training under a strict timeline (e.g. for the Physics Olympiad / IPhO), do not solve every question. The book contains many research-level questions. Instead, solve selectively with a mentor. If you don't have one, feel free to contact me—I keep a list of curated, high-value Irodov problems that target active exam topics.

Rule #1: Solution Discipline

Never see the solution first and come to the question. Always do the opposite. First come to the question and then go to the solution. It's a very simple thing, but still I am saying this.

When it comes to the solving part, first dissect the problems in this path: draw the physical scenario, identify constraints, trace conservative values, and construct equations only after you have developed a solid graphical visualization of the forces at play.

Resource review and coaching programs for advanced physics courses:

  • Art of Problem Solving (AoPS) Physics Highly recommended curriculum for international physics streams.
  • Vedantu Olympiad School (India) Personally enrolled; quite a nice setup as of date (July 5, 2026).

  • PhysicsWallah Affordable syllabus coverage for the initial stage of Indian Olympiads.
💡 True high achievers are autodidacts (self-learners). Don't worry about program brand names; just dedicate solid, focused time to the questions. Theory is just a blush.
